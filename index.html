Presentation overview

This study explores how personality traits are perceived in neural-rendered talking videos. Participants compare pairs of videos that differ in expression and head pose, then provide comparative judgments.

  • Data: paired videos with controlled expression differences
  • Measures: Big Five traits + perceived naturalness
  • Method: comparative judgment questions

üìå Research Motivation

Neural rendering techniques have revolutionized video synthesis, but questions remain about how these synthetic faces are perceived in terms of personality traits. This study investigates:

  • How do different facial expressions influence personality perception?
  • What role does head pose play in personality attribution?
  • Can neural-rendered faces convey believable personality traits?

üéØ Study Design

The study employs a comparative judgment approach where participants evaluate paired videos:

  • Stimuli: Neural-rendered talking head videos
  • Variables: Expression (angry, happy, sad, neutral, surprise, disgust)
  • Poses: Different head orientations and angles
  • Method: Pairwise comparison with forced-choice responses

üìä Personality Traits Measured

Based on the Big Five personality model:

  • Openness: Creativity, curiosity, open-mindedness
  • Conscientiousness: Organization, responsibility, discipline
  • Extraversion: Sociability, assertiveness, energy
  • Agreeableness: Kindness, cooperation, trust
  • Neuroticism: Emotional stability, anxiety levels
  • Naturalness: How realistic the video appears

üé• Video Dataset

The stimuli consist of carefully selected neural-rendered videos with controlled variations:

  • 128x128 resolution for consistent viewing
  • Multiple expression categories per pose
  • Paired presentation for comparative judgment
  • Audio removed to isolate visual perception

üë• Participant Task

Participants complete the following steps:

  • View two videos side by side
  • Answer: "Which person seems more [trait]?"
  • Provide comparative judgments (not absolute ratings)
  • Evaluate naturalness of each video
  • Progress through multiple video pairs

üî¨ Research Questions

  • Do happy expressions increase perceived extraversion?
  • Are neutral faces seen as more conscientious?
  • How does head pose modulate personality perception?
  • What is the relationship between naturalness and trait attribution?

üí° Expected Findings

Preliminary expectations based on literature:

  • Happy expressions ‚Üí higher extraversion ratings
  • Angry expressions ‚Üí lower agreeableness

üöÄ Future Directions

  • Compare neural-rendered vs. real video perception
  • Develop predictive models for personality attribution
  • Apply findings to avatar design and virtual agents
